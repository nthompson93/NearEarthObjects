Due to a White House mandate, agencies that are funded by the federal government must make their research publicly available. Thankfully, NASA has created an “Open Innovation” program that allows people to get creative with this public data. This site specifically makes use of NASA’s NeoWs (Near Earth Object Web Service), which provides data about near Earth objects based on the date that they are closest to the Earth.
Data is updated everday at 12am EST.

What exactly are Near Earth Objects? According to NASA, “(NEOs) are comets and asteroids that have been nudged by the gravitational attraction of nearby planets into orbits that allow them to enter the Earth’s neighborhood.” Asteroids are the rocky material that orbit the sun and are thought to be debris left over from the formation of the solar system. Comets are bodies composed mostly of water ice and frozen gasses, which have dust particles embedded in them. They are also thought to be debris from the solar system.

What makes a NEO potentially hazardous? At the moment, NEOs are deemed either potentially hazardous or non hazardous. To be potentially hazardous means that the NEO is within .05 Astronomical Units of Earth (4,647,790 miles), an absolute magnitude of 22 H - Solar System Bodies) or less, or have a diameter of at least 500 feet.
